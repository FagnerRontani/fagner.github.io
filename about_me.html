Profile

Agronomist with a Master’s degree in Agronomy and current Ph.D. student in Remote Sensing for Agriculture at North Dakota State University. 10+ years of academic and field experience in crop production, soil fertility, precision agriculture, and plant monitoring.

I work with multispectral sensors, UAVs, and RTK GNSS, turning drone & satellite imagery into actionable insights. Daily stack: Pix4D, QGIS, ArcGIS Pro, ENVI, spectral indices and raster analytics.

Passionate about transforming agricultural challenges into sustainable, data-driven solutions by combining solid technical expertise with hands-on field knowledge to support smart farming and better decision-making.

Regional experience in Brazil, the Southern United States (Louisiana), and the Northern Plains (North Dakota), adapting solutions to diverse soils and agroecosystems.

Research focus: integrating remote sensing with agronomy fundamentals to propose context-specific, sustainable recommendations for farmers and researchers.
